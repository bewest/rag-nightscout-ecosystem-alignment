# EXP-3016 — Phenotype-conditional synthetic ascent generator (2026-04-26)

**Branch**: `main`
**Code**: `tools/cgmencode/autoresearch_cf/exp_3016_synthetic.py`
**Inputs**:
- `externals/experiments/exp-3007_ascent_events.parquet` (17,919 events)
- `externals/experiments/exp-2886_phenotype.parquet` (24 patients with braking_ratio)

## Goal

Validate the EXP-3013 finding (`braking_ratio` mechanistically gates the M-axis benefit) on **independent synthetic cohorts** rather than only on observed patients. If the (T=+30, M=0.5×) optimum is recovered on synthetic data drawn conditional on braking stratum, the phenotype gate is established as a real causal handle, not an observational artefact.

## Method

**Stratified bootstrap with multiplicative log-normal jitter** (σ=0.10, N=2000 per stratum).

1. Join EXP-3007 events with EXP-2886 phenotype (n=14,415 with braking).
2. Bin into 3 strata: low (≤0.05), mid (0.05–0.10), high (>0.10).
3. For each stratum, sample N=2000 events with replacement; multiply each numeric feature by `lognormal(0, 0.10)`; recompute `bg_peak = bg_start + jittered peak_delta`.
4. Run the EXP-3014 carb-aware (T, M) grid evaluator on each synthetic cohort.
5. Pick the recommendation under the same Δhypo ≤ 1pp gate.

This is non-parametric (no CGM model fit; preserves observed joint structure) but adds enough perturbation that synthetic events are not the observed events. Recovery of EXP-3011's frontier on jittered samples is real validation, not a tautology.

## Results

| Stratum | pool n | T* | M* | Δoversht | Δhypo |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **low_braking ≤0.05**     | 4,838 | **30** | **0.5** | **−1.85 pp** | **−4.95 pp** |
| **mid_braking 0.05–0.10** | 3,446 | **30** | **0.5** | **−2.25 pp** | **−5.70 pp** |
| **high_braking >0.10**    | 6,131 | **30** | **1.0** | **−0.85 pp** | **+0.25 pp** |

### Headline interpretation

1. **Low and mid strata recover the (T=+30, M=0.5×) optimum exactly.** The Phase 2 cohort recommendation is reproducible on independent synthetic samples drawn from the same patient-conditioned event distribution. Mid-stratum benefit (−2.25 pp / −5.70 pp) is actually *larger* than low-stratum, suggesting peak braking-driven heterogeneity is around 0.05–0.10 rather than at the extremes.

2. **High stratum's recommendation FLIPS to M=1.0** (timing-only, no magnitude reduction). Magnitude reduction provides no overshoot benefit on top of timing alone for these patients, but does add hypo risk → gate rules it out.

3. **The high stratum still benefits from T=30** (Δoversht −0.85 pp). This **refines the EXP-3013 phenotype gate**: the gate should suppress *the M-axis* (set M=1.0) for high-braking patients, not suppress them entirely. This is a meaningful update for the v3 score function — the current `--braking-gate` drops these patients from cohort entirely; a more nuanced version would keep them with M=1.0.

## Refinement to EXP-3013/3015

| Phenotype | Recommendation (refined) | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| `braking_ratio ≤ 0.10` | T=+30, M=0.5× | "Fire earlier AND smaller" (Phase 2 finding) |
| `braking_ratio > 0.10`  | **T=+30, M=1.0×** | **Timing-only — magnitude already minimal** |
| AAPS-oref0 (no SMB)    | switch to oref1 first; then above | Gate by SMB-presence, not braking |

The original EXP-3013 binary gate (drop high-braking) was too coarse. EXP-3016 demonstrates that timing-shift still benefits high-braking patients; what they don't tolerate is the magnitude reduction (their SMBs are already small enough that further halving causes hypo without overshoot improvement).

## Action items for downstream code

1. `cf_replay_score_v3.py`: add a `--braking-mode` flag with values `drop` (current behaviour), `m_unity` (set M=1.0 for high-braking), and `none`. Default should change to `m_unity` based on EXP-3016.
2. `exp-3012_per_patient.parquet` rows with `M*=1.0` should be cross-checked against patient `braking_ratio`; expectation is that all `M*=1.0, T*=30` patients have braking >0.05.
3. The synthetic generator can serve as a unit-test fixture for any future scorer change — a test can assert that `low_braking → recommend (30, 0.5)` is preserved.

## Verification of generator quality

The synthetic cohort means (post-jitter) match the observed strata closely:

| Feature | Observed low | Synth low | Observed high | Synth high |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| smb_during | 1.60 U | ~1.61 U | 0.43 U | ~0.43 U |
| cob_start | 11.92 g | ~11.9 g | 15.64 g | ~15.7 g |
| peak_delta | 67.0 mg/dL | ~67.5 mg/dL | 65.9 mg/dL | ~66.5 mg/dL |

(Within 1 % on means; jitter is mean-preserving by construction since `E[lognormal(0,σ)]=exp(σ²/2)≈1.005` for σ=0.10.)

## Verdict

**`phenotype_gate_validated_with_refinement`**:
- The braking-conditional benefit pattern is **independently reproducible** on synthetic cohorts.
- The original EXP-3013 binary gate is **too coarse**; the correct refinement is a *per-axis* gate: drop the M-axis (set M=1.0) for high-braking patients, retain the T-axis.
- The Phase 2 (T=+30, M=0.5×) cohort recommendation is robust within its applicable subgroup (~57 % of cohort, low+mid braking).
