## Findings

1. **The post-prandial TIR gap exceeds the fasted gap in both
   significant tertiles.**
   - mid_cf: PP +27.80 vs FASTED +19.41 (PP is ~1.4× larger)
   - high_cf: PP +18.48 vs FASTED +11.13 (PP is ~1.7× larger)
   Pooled across cf, PP gap is ~27 pp vs FASTED gap ~17 pp.

2. **Loop's post-prandial TIR is 48.64 %** — about half of all
   post-prandial cells fall out of range. The TAR is 49.17 %.
   This is the dominant contributor to Loop's overall TIR
   deficit. EGP handling (FASTED 73.9 %) is comparatively OK.

3. **oref1's PP TIR is 75.81 %.** The mechanism stack —
   UAM detection + dynamic-ISF rising on bolus + SMB-as-
   correction during absorption — produces a 27-point structural
   advantage over Loop's brake-only post-prandial response.

4. **Even with autobolus, Loop's PP TIR is much lower than
   oref1's.** From EXP-2921 we know autobolus halves dawn-hyper
   but morning-hypo persists; this experiment shows autobolus
   alone does not close the larger PP gap. UAM and dynamic-ISF
   together likely matter more than autobolus alone for
   meal handling.

5. **oref0 PP TIR is 70.47 %** — only ~5 pp below oref1's
   75.81 %. So oref0's UAM + SMB-as-correction subsystem is
   competitive with oref1 on PP handling. The latency penalty
   shows up overnight, not after meals.

## Mechanism attribution

| Source of oref1 advantage | Estimated contribution |
|---------------------------|-----------------------:|
| PP carb handling (UAM + SMB + dynamic ISF response) | ~60 % |
| FASTED EGP handling (dynamic ISF widening) | ~40 % |

Both real, both significant. The carb-handling lever has the
larger absolute effect; the EGP lever has the cleaner causal
isolation (EXP-2922/2923/2924).

## Caveats

- low_cf cells with Loop n=1 cannot inferentially test the gap.
- `time_since_carb_min` capped at 360 — patients with truly long
  fasts all bin into FASTED.
- Cohort small (n=7 Loop, n=9 oref1). Direction and within-
  tertile significance both consistent.
- Mechanism attribution is correlation-based, not interventional.
  Future natural-experiment / counterfactual would tighten.

## Implication

For AID authors, the design priority order suggested by this
cohort is:

1. **Add UAM detection + SMB-as-correction during absorption**
   (largest TIR delta) — the post-prandial 50 % out-of-range
   rate in Loop is the single largest remediable burden in this
   data.
2. **Add dynamic-ISF for dawn EGP** (cleaner-mechanism delta) —
   smaller absolute effect but isolated, robust, and addresses
   a distinct physiologic challenge.
3. **Improve basal-cut latency** (oref0's residual gap) — the
   smallest absolute effect but lowest-effort fix for legacy
   oref0/openaps codebases.
