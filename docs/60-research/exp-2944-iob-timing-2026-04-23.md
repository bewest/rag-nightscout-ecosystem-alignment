# EXP-2944 — IOB-delta mechanism for the recovery gap

**Date**: 2026-04-23
**Audience**: AID code authors (decisive mechanism finding)

## Scope

Final in-grid mechanism test for the +21 pp Loop_AB_ON vs oref1
recovery gap during sustained-high carb-isolated windows. Tests
the **true `iob` column** (vs the EXP-2941 SMB-sum proxy which
missed basal-insulin contribution) plus `net_basal` deviation.

## What this is NOT

- Not therapy advice. Both Loop and oref1 designs are engineered
  trade-offs and patient choice/access dominates AID selection.
- Not a model of insulin pharmacokinetics — uses controller's
  reported IOB at 5-min granularity.

## Result — IOB DELTA is the mechanism

| Measure                           | Loop_AB_ON | oref1   | Δ      |
|-----------------------------------|-----------:|--------:|-------:|
| Recovery                          |   35.2%    |  55.4%  | −20.2% |
| `iob_start` (U at window entry)   |   2.515    |  1.973  | +0.542 |
| **`iob_delta` (U over 60-min)**   | **+0.588** |**−0.041**|**+0.629**|
| `net_basal_mean` (U/h deviation)  |  −1.013    | −0.698  | −0.315 |
| `basal_cut_frac` (cells cut)      |   0.938    |  0.946  | −0.008 |

### Within-design IOB-start tertile recovery

| Design     | lo IOB  | mid IOB  | hi IOB |
|------------|--------:|---------:|-------:|
| Loop_AB_ON | 0.357   |  0.353   |  0.347 |
| oref1      | 0.529   |  0.562   |  0.570 |

For Loop_AB_ON, starting IOB has zero effect on recovery (0.36 vs
0.35 vs 0.35). For oref1, starting IOB modestly helps (0.53 → 0.57).

## Mechanism interpretation

**Loop_AB_ON is still loading IOB during the recovery window**
(+0.588 U built up over 60 min). Its starting IOB is HIGHER than
oref1's (2.51 vs 1.97 U) but that IOB is bound in slow buildup,
not yet acting. The insulin Loop "has on board" is mostly *future*
insulin from recently-fired SMBs that haven't peaked yet.

**oref1 is IOB-neutral during the window** (−0.041 U). Its dose
was front-loaded BEFORE the window and is now actively working.
This matches:

- EXP-2940 time-to-BG-peak: oref1 22.9 min vs Loop_AB_ON 29.7 min
  (6.8-min faster peak)
- EXP-2937 sizing-lever: oref1 fires fewer SMBs (2.82 vs 4.31) at
  smaller per-event size but those SMBs landed earlier
- EXP-2930 PP front-loading: 2.2× dose density 0–30 min, 6.6× at
  30–60 min for oref1

**Basal cut is NOT the lever.** Both designs cut basal in 94% of
cells; mean cut depths differ slightly but both are aggressive.
The basal channel does not distinguish them.

The within-design tertile pattern confirms the timing diagnosis:

- For Loop, more starting IOB doesn't help because the IOB curve
  is still climbing (decline from IOB happens *after* the 60-min
  window ends). Recovery is timing-blocked, not IOB-blocked.
- For oref1, more starting IOB helps because the IOB is at its
  peak action during the window.

## Three-tier mechanism stack (UPDATED, post EXP-2944)

| Tier | Lever | Evidence | Effect |
|------|-------|----------|--------|
| 1. Channel availability | SMB-as-correction present | EXP-2942 (oref0≈Loop_AB_OFF at 30%) | +6 pp |
| 2. **IOB-delivery timing** | front-load vs back-load | **EXP-2944** (iob_delta +0.629 U) | **+20 pp** |
| 3. Dose-sizing logic | velocity/absorption-aware | EXP-2937 / EXP-2940 | (subsumed in tier 2) |

Tier 2 supersedes the prior "dose-sizing logic" framing. The
distinguishing feature is **WHEN the insulin lands relative to
the BG response window**, not the dose magnitude per se.

## AID-author actionable lever (REFINED)

The recovery edge comes from delivering insulin EARLIER in the
predicted-rise trajectory, so that IOB peaks DURING the recovery
window rather than after it. Concrete implications:

1. **Predict-and-fire on rising velocity**, not on already-elevated BG.
   oref1's UAM/dynamic-ISF triggers SMBs while BG is still rising;
   Loop_AB_ON fires after BG crosses thresholds.
2. **Larger early SMBs**, smaller late SMBs. Oref1 fires fewer
   SMBs total but front-loads aggressively.
3. **Time-to-peak is the metric.** Aim for IOB peak within 20–25 min
   of the predicted high crossing, not 35–45 min.

## Methodological correction to EXP-2941

EXP-2941 tested `prior_smb_3h` as an IOB proxy and found
distributions identical (Loop 2.749 vs oref1 3.072 U). This proxy
missed the basal-insulin component AND misframed the question:
**starting IOB is not the gap; iob_delta during the window is.**
Both designs have similar pre-window IOB; they differ in whether
that IOB is still climbing (Loop) or peaking (oref1).

The EXP-2941 selection-bias flag was therefore a measurement
artifact. Combined with EXP-2942 (cross-cohort match) and
EXP-2943 (η²=0.640), the selection-bias hypothesis is now closed.

## Carry-forward invariants

- **IOB timing > IOB magnitude** for sustained-high recovery.
  Two designs with equal starting IOB can have +20 pp recovery
  difference based on timing.
- **In-grid mechanism searches must use the `iob` column directly,
  not SMB-sum proxies.** Basal contribution is non-trivial.
- **basal_cut_frac is design-equivalent at sustained-high entries**
  (94% across Loop_AB_ON and oref1). Basal posture is not a
  correction-loop differentiator.

## Three-tier mechanism map (final, post-2944)

| Window               | Loop offence                | oref1 offence                |
|----------------------|----------------------------|------------------------------|
| PP 0-60min           | AB_ON only, late-fire      | UAM front-load               |
| PP 60-240min         | basal cut + smaller SMBs   | continued cadence            |
| Sustained-high entry | back-loaded IOB (+0.588 U during window) | front-loaded IOB (peaking) |
