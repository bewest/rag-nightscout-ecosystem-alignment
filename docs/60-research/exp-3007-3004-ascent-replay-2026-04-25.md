## EXP-3007 — Ascent extraction
17,919 ascent events extracted (ROC≥1.5 mg/dL/5min for ≥4 cells, peak_delta≥25 mg/dL).

| Controller | n events | overshoot rate | mean SMB during | % events with SMB |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **Loop**    | 6,004 | **60.0%** | 1.24 U | 63.0% |
| **Trio**    | 6,014 | **39.8%** | 1.43 U | 84.3% |
| OpenAPS (= AAPS-oref0) | 3,336 | 51.6% | 0.017 U | 0.6% |
| (unlabeled) | 2,565 | 25.3% | 1.03 U | 74.5% |

**This single table independently re-derives the IOB-age/SMB-mechanism capstone** from raw 5-min grid data, without leaning on the prior summary parquets. Loop fires fewer-but-bigger SMBs and tolerates more overshoot; Trio fires more-and-smaller SMBs and overshoots less; OpenAPS-AAPS-oref0 essentially never fires SMB and overshoots in between.

## EXP-3004 — Ascent counterfactual replay
For each ascent event, compute `cf_peak = bg_peak + smb_during × kernel_factor × isf` (oref0 PK, midpoint-of-ascent fraction). Result table:

| Controller | obs overshoot | cf overshoot | abs protection | n prevented | mean extra-drop attributed |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Loop    | 60.0% | 60.7% | **+0.73 pp** | 44 / 6004 | 2.36 mg/dL |
| Trio    | 39.8% | 41.6% | **+1.81 pp** | 109 / 6014 | 2.55 mg/dL |
| OpenAPS | 51.6% | 51.6% | +0.06 pp | 2 / 3336 | 0.08 mg/dL |
| (unlbl) | 25.3% | 27.0% | +1.72 pp | 44 / 2565 | 2.03 mg/dL |

## Big honest finding

**SMB-during-ascent contributes only 0.7-1.8 pp of overshoot prevention.**
The mean ascent window is ~37 min; the oref0 PK with peak=75 min realises only ~3 % of an SMB's insulin effect at the midpoint of that window. The peak occurs at the END of the ascent, so SMB has essentially no time to act on the peak itself.

This means **the Loop=60 % / Trio=40 % overshoot gap is intrinsic to control strategy, not driven by per-event SMB efficacy.** Loop tolerates higher peaks because (i) its application factor + AB strategy fires fewer SMBs, (ii) when it does fire SMB, it does so *later* (closer to the peak) and the bigger pulses *accelerate post-peak recovery* rather than prevent the peak itself.

## Re-framing the capstone hypothesis

| Original framing | Revised framing (post EXP-3004) |
|---|---|
| Loop = magnitude lever, Trio = frequency lever | Loop = magnitude *recovery* lever, Trio = frequency *prevention* lever |
| Both prevent overshoot | Trio prevents (high-frequency early SMB), Loop accepts overshoot then accelerates recovery |
| SMB efficacy ≈ kernel × ISF integrated | SMB-during-ascent efficacy on PEAK = kernel(ascent_midpoint→peak) × ISF (~3 % of nominal) |

The *recovery-acceleration* claim is testable: would the post-peak descent slope be steeper for Loop vs Trio events with similar peak BG? Defer to EXP-3013 (out of scope).

## Why this isn't a contradiction with EXP-3000-3003
Descent windows treat insulin already-onboard before the descent began (mean IOB at descent_start = 1.7 U); the kernel integrates over a long tail. Ascent windows treat insulin delivered *during* the rise; the kernel barely starts before the peak. Same engine, opposite ends of the PK curve — both honest.

## Verdict
**`capstone_partially_confirmed_with_reframe`** — the *observable* Loop-vs-Trio overshoot asymmetry is reproduced from raw grid data (EXP-3007), but the cf-replay attribution shows SMB-during-ascent does *not* mechanistically explain it. The capstone's mechanism is therefore best stated as a **strategy difference (when to fire) rather than a per-SMB efficacy difference**.
