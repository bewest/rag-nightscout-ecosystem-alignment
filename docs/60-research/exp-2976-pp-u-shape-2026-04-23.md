# EXP-2976 — PP-context U-shape of SMB-velocity slope vs BG band

**Date**: 2026-04-23
**Audience**: Open-source AID code authors
**Scope**: Quadratic U-shape test of SMB-on-velocity slope across BG
bands in **post-prandial** context (carbs in prior 60 min, none current
cell), repeated from EXP-2975's no-carb design.
**What this is NOT**: per-patient therapy advice; not a TIR claim.

## Hypothesis

EXP-2975 found a U-shape in no-carb context — SMB-on-velocity slope
fell from 70-100 toward target then rose back at high BG. The
hypothesis was that meal-state would FLIP curvature (no defensive
backoff at high BG when controller knows carbs are pending).

## Result — NEGATIVE / NULL

Both designs **preserve U-shape under PP**:

| Design     | EXP-2975 (no-carb) `c` | vertex | EXP-2976 (PP) `c`  | vertex |
|------------|-----------------------:|-------:|-------------------:|-------:|
| Loop_AB_ON | +9.4e-6 (z=+9.2)       | 214    | **+8.7e-6** (z=+5.0) | 236    |
| oref1      | +2.8e-6 (z=+3.15)      | 347    | **+3.5e-6** (z=+2.83) | 322    |

Curvature sign and magnitude are essentially unchanged. Both
designs maintain a U-shape across meal context; the carb-pending
state does not flatten or invert the high-BG defensive arm.

Per-band slopes (PP) are uniformly higher than no-carb (Loop 85→0.43
vs no-carb baseline ~0.27; oref1 85→0.42), confirming PP context
intensifies SMB-on-velocity coupling, but the SHAPE remains U.

n = 144,931 PP windows (Loop_AB_ON 20,550; oref1 62,533; rest oref0/AB_OFF inactive).

## Interpretation for AID authors

- The U-shape (low at near-target, high at edges) is a **persistent
  controller signature**, not a no-carb artifact.
- Loop's sharper U remains sharper in PP (vertex 236 vs oref1 322 OOR).
- "Carbs flatten the defense" — REJECTED. PP does not switch off
  high-BG defensive backoff in either design.
- For controller refinement: meal-state-conditional gain shaping
  (Lever 3 in synthesis) **should preserve a U-shape design**, not
  expect a monotone dose-response.
