# EXP-2916 — Controller-design protection-gap characterisation

**Date:** 2026-04-23 (overnight)
**Source:** `tools/cgmencode/exp_migration_delta_2916.py`
**Scope:** Scientific characterisation of head-to-head AID controller
design differences for **open-source AID author audiences**. NOT a
per-patient recommendation surface.

## Scope statement (binding)

This experiment quantifies how much per-cell severe-event protection
differs across AID controller designs at the same load tercile,
expressed as the per-patient exposure each design's gap would imply.
**It is not a recommendation that any individual patient migrate
between AID systems.** Device choice depends on access, hardware,
support, family/HCP context, and patient autonomy — all of which sit
outside this dataset. The framing throughout is "design A delivers X
protection at this load tier; design B delivers Y; here is the
implied delta" — a comparative-design metric, not therapy advice.

The intended downstream audiences are:
1. Open-source AID authors deciding which design gaps to close
2. Researchers comparing closed-loop architectures
3. The deconfounding toolkit (capacity-vs-utilization decomposition,
   §2.10), as a calibration check on Guard-#6 verified rankings

## Method (one paragraph)

For each patient `p` with own (lineage, tercile, cf_severe,
own_protection), look up the cell-mean protection in alternative
lineages at the same tercile (using the Guard-#6-verified EXP-2904
cell means). Compute `design_gap_exposure = (alt_protection −
own_protection) × cf_severe`. Positive value indicates the patient's
current AID design is below the alternative design's mean at the
same load tier; magnitude scales with the patient's load.

## Headline (controller-design level)

**Per-cell protection differences between designs are large enough
to surface in 8 of 19 patient-load combinations at ≥5pp.**

| Own design     | n | Cells below ≥1 alt | Mean signed gap |
|----------------|--:|-------------------:|----------------:|
| oref0 (legacy) | 3 | 2                  | +0.194          |
| Loop (iOS)     | 7 | 5                  | +0.091          |
| oref1 (modern) | 9 | 1                  | -0.041          |

Direction matches Guard-#6 verified ranking from EXP-2904: oref1 cell
means form the upper envelope of the protection surface; Loop sits
below; oref0 sits below both at the conservative tier.

## Largest design gaps (controller-design comparison)

| Cell                              | Reference design (alt) | Gap   |
|-----------------------------------|------------------------|------:|
| oref0 conservative                | oref1 conservative     | +0.51 |
| oref0 moderate                    | Loop moderate / oref1  | +0.25 |
| Loop conservative                 | oref1 conservative     | +0.18 |

These describe **design-level deltas**, weighted by the load-intensity
distribution of patients currently on each design.

## Critical caveat: ALL oref0 cells are n=1

The oref0 cohort contains three patients, one per tercile. Every
oref0 "cell mean" is therefore a single-patient point estimate.
The design-level claims about oref0 in this report are best read as
**three case studies, not a design-cohort comparison**:

| Cell                | n | Patient        | Notes                   |
|---------------------|--:|----------------|-------------------------|
| oref0 conservative  | 1 | odc-86025410   | mechanism_gap regime    |
| oref0 moderate      | 1 | odc-96254963   | average performer       |
| oref0 aggressive    | 1 | odc-74077367   | manual-SMB over-performer (EXP-2905) |

The headline oref0–oref1 conservative gap (0.51) is therefore "one
oref0 patient at 0.13 vs three oref1 patients averaging 0.63." That
gap is mechanistically diagnosed (EXP-2892 basal-cut utilisation 20 %
vs 92 %), so the direction is corroborated independently — but the
magnitude is one observation deep on the oref0 side. AAPS data
ingestion (EXP-2908) is the cleanest path to widening this base.

## Secondary caveat: oref0-aggressive cell is a manual-SMB outlier

The "best alt = oref0" arm in the raw output is driven entirely by
odc-74077367, the over_performer_at_load patient (EXP-2905) who
hand-compensates SMB via 98.6 % user_bolus and stack 0.75. This is
**not a property of oref0 the algorithm** — it is a single user's
manual-substitution behaviour. Any cross-design analysis must flag
oref0-aggressive as low-confidence (n=1) and exclude that cell as a
"design reference" for protection.

After excluding the artifact cell, the design-comparison surface
collapses to: **oref1 forms the design ceiling at every tercile**.
This is consistent with EXP-2892 (basal-cut utilisation 92 % vs
oref0 20 %), EXP-2893 (SMB channel presence), and EXP-2894 (Loop
auto-bolus optionality).

## What this means for AID authors

- **oref0 conservative-tier basal-cut utilisation** is the largest
  design-level protection gap in the dataset (~0.51 vs oref1).
  Already mechanistically diagnosed in EXP-2892 as utilisation, not
  capacity. This gap motivates code changes to oref0's basal-cut
  responsiveness (or backporting oref1's basal-cut policy).
- **Loop-conservative** sits ~0.18 below oref1-conservative. Could
  reflect (a) auto-bolus opt-in fraction (EXP-2894), (b) SMB-size
  differences, or (c) cohort self-selection. EXP-2894 partly closes
  this; deeper mechanism work pending AAPS data (EXP-2908).
- **oref1 sets the ceiling** at all three terciles in this cohort.
  Other designs informing future work should compare against oref1
  cell means as the empirical reference.

## What this is NOT

- Not a patient-migration recommendation surface
- Not therapy advice
- Not a claim that any patient would benefit from changing AID
- Not a substitute for HCP / patient device-choice conversations
- Not actionable as a setting change for any individual

## Cohort caveats (carry forward)

- Per-cell n=1–4. Bootstrap CIs deferred to EXP-2917.
- No AAPS patients (per EXP-2908). Cannot separate "oref1 algorithm"
  from "Trio iOS platform" in the design-level claim.
- Claim is bounded to the load-intensity envelope of this cohort.

## Audition matrix implications

**Withdrawn:** the originally-proposed `algorithm_migration_candidate`
audition flag. Patient-level audition flags should not advise device
migration.

**Kept (already wired):**
- `regime_mechanism_gap` — flags patients whose protection deficit is
  not explainable by load saturation (independently of design)
- `regime_load_saturation` — flags patients whose deficit is
  load-mediated; remediation is settings de-aggression
- `lax_braking_controller_efficacy` — flags weak-braking patients;
  remediation is setting/configuration review

These flags surface mechanism without prescribing a device change.
