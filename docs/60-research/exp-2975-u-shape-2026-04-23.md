# EXP-2975 — Formal U-shape test of SMB-slope vs BG band (2026-04-23)

## Scope
For open-source AID code authors. EXP-2966 hinted that the SMB-on-
velocity slope is **highest at the 70-100 mg/dL band** and **lowest
near 200 mg/dL**, with a small re-rise at the highest bands —
suggesting a U-shape. Formally tests by fitting a quadratic
`slope ~ a + b·BG + c·BG²` to the per-band slopes and reporting
whether `c > 0` (positive curvature = U).

## What this is NOT
Not therapy advice. Not a per-patient finding.

## Method
For each (design, BG-band, no-carb) cell, fit pooled
`ins_60_smb ~ vel_30` to obtain a per-band slope and SE (cell-level
n ≥ 30). Then fit a weighted (1/SE²) OLS quadratic across BG-band
midpoints, per design. Report `c` with z-statistic and the vertex
BG (= −b / 2c) of the quadratic.

## Headline
**POSITIVE — U-shape confirmed for both designs, with Loop's curve
~3.4× more pronounced.**

- **Loop_AB_ON**: significant positive curvature (`c = +9.4e-6`,
  z = +9.2, p ≈ 0). Vertex (slope minimum) at **BG ≈ 214 mg/dL**,
  inside the analyzed range. SMB-on-velocity slope is highest at
  the low edge (85 mg/dL: +0.86), troughs at 200 mg/dL (+0.69),
  and rises modestly back to +0.79 at 280.
- **oref1**: smaller but still significant positive curvature
  (`c = +2.8e-6`, z = +3.15, p = 0.00165). Vertex at **BG ≈ 347
  mg/dL**, *outside* the analyzed range — meaning the U-shape's
  rising arm is barely engaged within the bands we measured. The
  slope decreases approximately monotonically from 85 mg/dL
  (+0.57) to ≈ 200-280 mg/dL (~+0.42), with no meaningful
  re-engagement.

## Interpretation for AID authors

- Both designs show **strongest velocity coupling near target** and
  weakened coupling at moderate hyperglycemia (180-220 mg/dL). This
  is the **target-band suppression** previously hypothesized:
  controllers are tuned to be most reactive when the patient is
  near-recovery from low BG (where momentum is the only signal),
  and less reactive in the "obvious correction" mid-high band where
  the basal-cap and insulin-already-on-board damp marginal SMB
  decisions.
- **Loop's stronger curvature** comes from the high-end re-rise
  (260-300 mg/dL slope +0.79 ≈ near the 70-100 peak). This is
  consistent with Loop's `units = (predictedBG − target)/ISF`
  scaling linearly in BG: at very high BG with rising velocity,
  the predicted overshoot is large enough to engage the application
  factor at full strength.
- **oref1's weaker curvature / out-of-range vertex** is consistent
  with its `microBolus = min(insulinReq/2, maxBolus)` cap: at high
  BG the cap binds, suppressing further velocity scaling.
